It is noted that there is a difference in the consumer purposes of gold between industrialized and non-industrial countries. In the United States and European countries, for example, industrial consumption outperforms domestic consumption, says Dr. Qalawun. Gold is used for many industrial purposes, such as making computers, smart phones, medical equipment, space exploration equipment, etc. In Arab countries, for example, “domestic consumption is superior to industrial consumption, as gold is used in weddings and gifts, and is considered a small wealth and a safe haven for saving, especially for women,” says Dr. Qalawun.
